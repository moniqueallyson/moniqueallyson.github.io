Education from UC Santa Barbara

On this page, I focus on the projects that stem from my life in academia as a vegan person and a yogi enthusiast. I have been challenged by my professors to utilize my passion and skills to create meaningful projects that can extend outside the classroom into the work field without sacrificing ourselves as individuals. Thankfully, I have many passions, interests, hobbies, and dreams to generate multiple projects throughout my years at UCSB.

Project 1

Why Veganism Matters

For my Critical Thinking course (ES 40), we were tasked to do a research project of our choice and interest throughout the entire quarter. As a person who was majoring in environmental studies, I was open to hearing from classmates and other peers about their dietary choices and conflicting environmental beliefs. I did research on the impact that both the agricultural and livestock industry have on the environment and the choices individuals can make to lessen it from their own home. I provided perspectives from different backgrounds, both cultural and economical through personal interviews as to provide insight on the barriers that may be preventing environmentally conscious people from lessening their animal-based choices. I also highlight local resources and aid for those who are seeking to start their own personal journey of reducing their carbon foot-print or just lower their animal-based food intake.

Yoga to Manage Chronic Pain

For my Data Science class, we were tasked with logging personal data from our lives over the course of the quarter (roughly 30 days). I decided to analyze my yoga routine and how it affects my chronic back pain. I tracked the days I did or didnâ€™t practice yoga and recorded my pain levels using a scale from 1 to 10.

Overall, the project was a powerful motivation to care for both my mental and physical health while learning how to work with real data. Iâ€™ve found that yoga and consistent movement can significantly reduce my pain. Unsurprisingly, the data showed that the days I skipped yoga were also the days I experienced the highest levels of discomfort.

library(tidyverse)
library(janitor)
library(plotly)
library(htmlwidgets)


mydata <- read.csv("Data.csv") |>
  clean_names() |>
  mutate(
    tooltip_text = paste0(
      "Yoga Session: ", did_you_do_yoga, "<br>",
      "Pain Level After: ", pain_level_after)
  )

p <- ggplot(mydata, aes(
  x = did_you_do_yoga,
  y = pain_level_after,
  color = pain_level_after,
  text = tooltip_text
)) +
  geom_jitter(width = 0.15, size = 2.5, alpha = 0.6) +
  stat_summary(geom = "point", fun = mean, color = "#4a4a4a", size = 3) +
  scale_color_gradient(low = "#f9f871", high = "#7c3f98") +  # yellow to purple gradient
  labs(
    x = "Did You Do Yoga?",
    y = "Pain Level After Yoga",
    color = "Pain Level"
  ) +
  theme_minimal(base_size = 13) +
  theme(
    plot.background = element_rect(fill = "#ffe6eb", color = NA),        # baby pink background
    panel.background = element_rect(fill = "#ffffff", color = "#ffb6c1"), # white panel with pink border
    panel.grid.major = element_line(color = "#ffccd5"),
    panel.grid.minor = element_blank()
  )

ggplotly(p, tooltip = "text") |>
  layout(
    hoverlabel = list(font = list(size = 14)),
    annotations = list())
